The handoff process
Demand Gen → SDR → AE → Client Concierge.
Three clean handoffs. Each one transfers full context. For inbound leads the journey starts earlier: ads drive landing-page visits, form fills become MQLs, the SDR qualifies, the AE closes. No member should ever feel like they're starting over. What travels with the member at every stage is as important as the handoff itself.

Handoff principle
No member should ever have to repeat themselves.
Every handoff transfers context, not just contact details. The SDR note sets up the AE. The AE recording sets up the Concierge. By the time a member speaks to their Concierge for the first time, the Concierge already knows their name, their goals, their concerns, and what made them say yes. That's the standard.

LWH SWOT
Strengths
  • Only Northern Ontario clinic combining in-person diagnostic depth with a daily AI health companion
  • Clara is a product-in-waiting, no competitor has built this, and LWH owns it from day one
  • Longevity Circuit covers 5 stations, 100+ biomarkers in a single day, operationally rare
  • 12-month membership creates recurring revenue, renewal mechanics, and genuine retention incentive
  • Three-pillar model (Medical + Clara + Community) creates compounding member value no single-track competitor can match
  • B2C and B2B motions running simultaneously with a clear phase progression to Clara subscription
  • 5,000 member cap protects service quality and brand premium at scale
Weaknesses
  • New brand, no established trust or outcomes evidence in Northern Ontario yet
  • Clara's member journey and daily engagement model are not yet fully mapped
  • Community track touchpoints (newsletter, events, programming) are still to be developed
  • Single location, geographic reach limited to Greater Sudbury and surrounding area initially
  • Break-even at 2,500 members means early revenue pressure before proof is established
  • Sales team is being built, no pipeline, CRM, or playbooks fully operational yet
  • No outcomes data to anchor B2B employer ROI conversations in Phase 1
Opportunities
  • Northern Ontario executive health market is entirely uncontested, NEOMO has no ongoing membership
  • Employer benefit channel via mining, industrial, and public sector employers is large and warm
  • Clara subscription as a standalone product creates a second revenue line and a scalable digital business beyond the clinic
  • Unions and Indigenous communities represent underserved partnership channels with long-term relationship potential
  • Broker and benefits advisor channel can multiply B2B reach without linear SDR headcount
  • Global longevity economy tailwind, $600B+ market, consumer awareness accelerating
  • Strategic evolution into MRI full-body scan and AI-enhanced imaging creates a premium add-on tier
Threats
  • NEOMO owns the "Northern Ontario executive health" category name today with existing employer relationships
  • Southern Ontario clinics (Medcan, La Vie, Harrison) could extend north via virtual care or satellite
  • US longevity brands (Function Health, Forward, Parsley Health) exploring Canadian market entry
  • Ontario regulatory environment, messaging must stay clearly in the prevention lane
  • Talent scarcity in Northern Ontario, physicians, exercise physiologists, dietitians harder to recruit than in Toronto
  • HSA and employer benefit budget already allocated to Teladoc or similar may limit room for LWH
  • Clara's subscription timeline is contingent on outcomes evidence that doesn't exist yet
